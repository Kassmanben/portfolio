Every day, when you go out into the world, you impact people whether you think you do or not. Some days, you spend hours supporting or caring for another person. Many days, maybe more than you are willing to admit – you forget to focus your attention on caring for yourself.

"Self-care" is a frequently used buzzword and yet, many people don’t really know what it means. Self-care is any activity that you do to intentionally take care of your mental, emotional & physical health and needs. And it is often overlooked. It is not something that you force yourself to do, or do out of obligation. It is anything but a one-time act. It is a mindset and a regular practice.

SELF-CARE STEPS TO TRY

  1. Call someone you trust to talk about your day
  2. Say ‘no’ to chores, obligations or “shoulds” that are less of a priority
  3. Have a good, long cry
  4. Go to bed earlier than usual
  5. Watch a mindless, funny, exciting TV show or movie
  6. Briefly feed your Youtube addiction – baby animals, brief histories, or practical jokes… enjoy!
  7. Reread a favorite book
  8. Listen, sing or dance to your favorite musical artist
  9. Reread your favorite picture and chapter books from childhood.
  10. Put clean sheets on your bed
  11. Make a nourishing meal that you are proud of
  12. Ask for help – at work, home, from a loved one or from a stranger
  13. Shower or take a bath – put on clothes that feel good
  14. Move your body for 20-30 minutes – stretch, dance, exercise, work out
  15. Take a tech break – deactivate social media for a bit
  16. Meditate, or just daydream & stare into space
  17. Drink a big glass of water and stay hydrated
  18. Go outside and get some sunshine or fresh air
  19. Write it out – journal, letter, or just a “brain dump”
  20. Make art – color, paint, work with clay, take photos… create!
  21. Go to a support group - connect with others you relate to
  22. See a doctor – if you suspect something is wrong, talk to a professional
  23. Get dirty – garden, play in the dirt
  24. Set up a chair and watch the sunrise or sunset or the starry sky
  25. Book a session with a therapist – it’s okay to want to feel better

Whatever you are going through – you will be okay. Remember that you have overcome things in the past. Remember that you have accomplished many things. Remember that you are not alone. The list above is just a starting point meant to prompt your own thinking about how you can best take care of yourself - during tough times as well as not-so-tough times. YOU MATTER – show yourself this is true!

How are you taking care of yourself today, this week, this month?
